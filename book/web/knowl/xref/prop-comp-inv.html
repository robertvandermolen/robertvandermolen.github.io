Proposition 7.2.8.

Let \(A\text{,}\) \(B\text{,}\) \(C\) and \(D\) be sets. Let \(R\) be a relation from \(A\) to \(B\text{,}\) \(S\) a relation from \(B\) to \(C\text{,}\) and \(T\) be a relation from \(C\) to \(D\)
  1. \(\displaystyle \left(R^{-1}\right)^{-1}=R\)
  2. \(T\circ(S\circ R)=(T\circ S)\circ R\) (composition is associative)
  3. \(I_B\circ R=R\) and \(R\circ I_A=R\)
  4. \(\displaystyle (S\circ R)^{-1}=R^{-1}\circ S^{-1}\)
