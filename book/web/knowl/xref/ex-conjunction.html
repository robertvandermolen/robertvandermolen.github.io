Example 1.1.2. Some Conjunction Examples.

  1. \(P\) : "It is raining outside."
    \(Q\) : "I have an umbrella."
    \(P\wedge Q \) : It is raining outside and I have an umbrella.
  2. \(P\text{:}\) "Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci was born in Italy."
    \(Q\text{:}\) "\(\frac{\pi}{2}\) is rational."
    \(P\wedge Q\text{:}\) "Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci was born in Italy and \(\frac{\pi}{2}\) is rational."
  3. \(P\text{:}\) "DNA stores information about how to build cells"
    \(Q\text{:}\) "Archaea are prokaryotes"
    \(P\wedge Q\text{:}\) "DNA stores information about how to build cells and Archaea are prokaryotes"
