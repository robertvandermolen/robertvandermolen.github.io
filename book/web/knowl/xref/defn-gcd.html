Definition 4.1.2. Greatest Common Divisor.

\(\forall a,b,c\in\Z\text{,}\) all non-zero, we call \(c\) the greatest common divisor of \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) denoted \(c=\text{GCD}(a,b)\) iff
  1. \(c\) is a common divisor of \(a\) and \(b\)
  2. every common divisor of \(d\in\Z\) of \(a\) and \(b\) has the property \(d\leq c\text{.}\) (every other divisor is smaller)
