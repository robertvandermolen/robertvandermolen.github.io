Example 1.5.3.

The predicates \(P(x): x^2+2x=-1\) and \(Q(x):x\lt 1\) are equivalent in the universe \(\mathcal{U}=\mathbb{W}\text{.}\) They are not equivalent in the universe \(\mathcal{U}=\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\)
To see this is true notice that with the universe \(\mathcal{U}=\mathbb{W}\) both \(P(x)\) and \(Q(x)\) have no elements which make it true, that is both of their truth sets are empty.
On the other hand for the universe \(\mathcal{U}=\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) \(P(x)\) has the truth set of \(\{-1\}\) yet \(Q(x)\) has the truth set of \(\{..., -3,-2,-1,0\}\) and these two sets are clearly not the same.
