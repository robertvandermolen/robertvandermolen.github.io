The logical disjunction is often referred to as the inclusive or, as it is still true when both propositions are true. In English we often assume the use of the exclusive or, that is, when we use the word or we most often mean only one of two choices. For example "would you like chicken or steak." When someone says this to you, you know immediately they do not mean that you can have both chicken and steak, that is not the case for the logical disjunction.
