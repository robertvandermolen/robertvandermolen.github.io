Modus Ponens.

Modus Ponens is a specific type of argument with two premises: \(P\implies Q\text{,}\) and \(P\text{,}\) and conludes \(Q\text{.}\) That is
\(P\implies Q\)
\(P\)
\(\therefore Q\)
An example of this is "If it is raining then the ground is wet. It is raining. Therefore the ground is wet."
