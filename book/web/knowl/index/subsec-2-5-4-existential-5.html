Example 1.5.2.

For this example lets consider the following predicates
  1. \(\displaystyle P(x): x \text{ loves books}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle Q(x): x \text{ eats pizza}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle R(x): x \gt 4\)
For (C) the statement \(\exists x\text{ }R(X)\) is true in the universe \(\mathcal{U}=\mathbb{R}\text{,}\) as \(4.5\in\mathbb{R}\) as well as many other numbers. In statement (A) there are no real numbers that have any opinions on books from my understanding, but if the universe was all people then \(\exists x\text{ }P(x)\) is true as I am a person and I love books. Similarly for (B) the proposition, \(\exists x\text{ }Q(x)\) in the universe of all people is also true, as I also eat pizza!
