For examples (B) and (C) in Example 1.1.3 are not propositions because (B) depends on who "She" is to determine its truth value, while in (C) it depends on what "\(x\)" is, for example when \(x\) is 2 it is True, but when \(x\) is 3 it is False. Example (A) from Example 1.1.3 is an exclamation and is neither True nor False. Finally, (D) from Example 1.1.3 is known as a paradox. If the statement “This sentence is false” is true, then by its meaning it must be false. On the other hand, if the given statement is false, then what it claims is false, so it must be true.
