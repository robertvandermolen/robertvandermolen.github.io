Discrete Probability Distribution consists of the values a random variable can assume and the corresponding probabilities of the values. The probabilities are determined theoretically or by observation.

Building a Discrete Distribution:

Step 1

Make a frequency table for the outcomes of the variable

Step 2

Find the probability for each outcome by dividing the frequency of the outcome by the sum of the frequencies.

Step 3

If a graph is required, place the outcomes on the horizontal axis and the probabilities on the vertical axis, and draw vertical bars for each outcome and its corresponding probability.

Does my distribution make sense?
  • The sum of the probabilities of all the events in the sample space must equal 1; that is, \(\sum P(x_i)=1\)
  • The probability of each event in the sample space must be between or equal to 0 and 1; that is, \(0\leq P(x_i)\leq1\)
